# Content Outline - Legal Writing 101 Simulation

## Scene Flow Overview
**Total Duration:** 8-10 minutes
**Interaction Style:** Branching scenarios with consequence tracking
**Core Thread:** Redesigning Legal Writing 101 from traditional to blended learning

## Scene 1: The Problem Brief (1 minute)
**Setting:** JUS conference room
**Characters:** Sarah Kim (Learning Director), Learner
**Objective:** Establish the redesign challenge
**Content:**
- Sarah presents current Legal Writing 101 problems
- Baseline data: 45% completion, 2.1/5 satisfaction
- Learner feedback: "too theoretical," "can't apply to real cases"
**Interaction:** Review data dashboard, identify top 3 problems
**Variables Set:** problem_focus (engagement/application/relevance)

## Scene 2: Analyze Learner Personas (1.5 minutes)
**Setting:** Research workspace with persona cards
**Characters:** Maya, Robert, Patricia (persona videos), ALEX (AI assistant)
**Objective:** Choose primary learner focus for redesign
**Content:**
- 30-second persona introduction videos
- Needs analysis for each learner type
- ALEX suggests design implications
**Interaction:** Select primary persona, justify choice
**Variables Set:** primary_persona, design_priority

## Scene 3: Define Design Framework (1.5 minutes)
**Setting:** Design studio with framework posters
**Characters:** ALEX (AI assistant), Learner
**Objective:** Select guiding design methodology
**Content:**
- ADDIE: Systematic analysis → design → development
- SAM: Rapid prototyping with stakeholder feedback
- Learning Experience Canvas: Co-creation with learners
**Interaction:** Choose framework, see preview of process
**Variables Set:** design_framework, methodology_focus

## Scene 4: Choose Blended Modality (1.5 minutes)
**Setting:** Digital workspace with modality options
**Characters:** Learner, ALEX
**Objective:** Decide delivery method for Legal Writing 101
**Content:**
- Option A: Workshop + pre-work videos
- Option B: Fully digital microlearning series
- Option C: Hybrid - digital cases + live peer review
**Interaction:** Drag-and-drop time/engagement/cost sliders
**Variables Set:** modality_choice, delivery_method

## Scene 5: Prototype Course Flow (2 minutes)
**Setting:** Interactive course builder interface
**Characters:** Learner, ALEX (provides suggestions)
**Objective:** Build Legal Writing 101 structure
**Content:**
- Course components: video cases, practice drafting, AI feedback, live debrief
- Sequencing options based on chosen framework
- Real-time effectiveness scoring
**Interaction:** Drag-and-drop course elements, arrange sequence
**Variables Set:** course_structure, effectiveness_score

## Scene 6: Stakeholder Review (2 minutes)
**Setting:** Virtual meeting room
**Characters:** Sarah Kim, David Thompson, Lisa Park
**Objective:** Incorporate stakeholder feedback
**Content:**
- Sarah: "Will this scale across departments?"
- David: "Does it preserve legal accuracy and standards?"
- Lisa: "Can we brand this as JUS Academy innovation?"
**Interaction:** Address each concern, revise design
**Variables Set:** stakeholder_satisfaction, design_revisions

## Scene 7: Analytics & Reflection (1 minute)
**Setting:** Data dashboard interface
**Characters:** Learner, ALEX
**Objective:** Evaluate design with projected metrics
**Content:**
- Projected engagement: 78% completion
- Writing quality improvement: 34% better clarity scores
- Cost-effectiveness analysis
**Interaction:** Interpret dashboard, answer reflection questions
**Variables Set:** success_metrics, reflection_responses

## Scene 8: Wrap-Up & Replay (0.5 minutes)
**Setting:** JUS conference room
**Characters:** Sarah Kim
**Objective:** Summarize achievement, offer replay
**Content:**
- "Your redesigned Legal Writing 101 approved for pilot"
- Summary of choices and outcomes
- Invitation to try different approach
**Interaction:** View summary report, choose replay option
**Variables Set:** completion_status, replay_interest

## Branching Logic
- Persona choice affects available modality options
- Framework choice influences prototype tools
- Stakeholder feedback varies based on previous choices
- Analytics reflect cumulative design decisions

## Assessment Integration
- Knowledge checks embedded in Scenes 2, 3, 5
- Reflection questions in Scenes 4, 6, 7
- Final competency demonstration in Scene 8
