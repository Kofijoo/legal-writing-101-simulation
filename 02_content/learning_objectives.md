# Learning Objectives - Legal Writing 101 Simulation

## Primary Learning Outcome
Demonstrate design thinking, process facilitation, and ability to connect business needs with learning outcomes through redesigning a traditional legal writing course.

## Specific Learning Objectives

### 1. Gap Analysis (Scene 1-2)
**Objective:** Identify performance and learning gaps in traditional Legal Writing 101 course
**Measurable Outcome:** Learner correctly identifies 3+ specific problems with current course format
**Assessment:** Multiple choice questions about course effectiveness

### 2. Framework Selection (Scene 3)
**Objective:** Select appropriate learning design frameworks for legal education context
**Measurable Outcome:** Learner chooses framework and provides 2+ justifications specific to legal writing
**Assessment:** Branching scenario with rationale explanation

### 3. Modality Design (Scene 4-5)
**Objective:** Prototype blended solutions tailored to diverse legal learners
**Measurable Outcome:** Learner creates course structure addressing 2+ persona needs
**Assessment:** Drag-and-drop interaction with feedback on design choices

### 4. Stakeholder Management (Scene 6)
**Objective:** Evaluate stakeholder feedback and revise design accordingly
**Measurable Outcome:** Learner incorporates 3+ stakeholder concerns into revised design
**Assessment:** Decision-making scenario with consequence tracking

### 5. Data-Driven Iteration (Scene 7-8)
**Objective:** Use analytics to inform design decisions
**Measurable Outcome:** Learner identifies 2+ metrics for measuring course success
**Assessment:** Dashboard interpretation and reflection questions

## Bloom's Taxonomy Alignment
- **Remember:** Course design terminology and frameworks
- **Understand:** Learner persona needs and constraints
- **Apply:** Design principles to legal education context
- **Analyze:** Stakeholder feedback and data insights
- **Evaluate:** Design effectiveness and trade-offs
- **Create:** Blended learning solution prototype

## Success Criteria
- 80% completion rate on all interactive elements
- Correct identification of design principles in final assessment
- Demonstration of learner-centered design thinking throughout simulation
