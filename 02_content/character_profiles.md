# Character Profiles - Legal Writing 101 Simulation

## Learner Personas (Course Users)

### 1. Maya Chen - Young Associate
**Role:** 2nd-year associate at mid-size firm
**Age:** 27
**Background:** Law school graduate, limited practical writing experience
**Pain Points:**
- Struggles with concise, persuasive writing
- Needs quick, actionable feedback
- Prefers digital, mobile-friendly learning
**Learning Preferences:**
- Scenario-based practice
- Peer collaboration
- Immediate feedback
**Quote:** "I need to write briefs that actually persuade judges, not just show I read cases."

### 2. Robert Martinez - Senior Partner
**Role:** 15-year litigation partner
**Age:** 42
**Background:** Experienced lawyer, mentors junior staff
**Pain Points:**
- Limited time for training
- Wants proven, credible content
- Needs to see ROI on learning investment
**Learning Preferences:**
- Efficiency-focused
- Real case examples
- Expert-led content
**Quote:** "Show me how this makes my team write better briefs, faster."

### 3. Judge Patricia Williams - Judicial Mentor
**Role:** Federal district judge, guest instructor
**Age:** 58
**Background:** 20+ years bench experience, teaches legal writing
**Pain Points:**
- Sees poor writing quality in submissions
- Values traditional rigor with modern relevance
- Expects professional standards
**Learning Preferences:**
- Standards-based approach
- Historical context
- Practical application
**Quote:** "Lawyers must write clearly. Lives and livelihoods depend on it."
